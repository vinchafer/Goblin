# Max Audit — v2 (2026-06-02)

**Re-audit after the model fix, this time as a genuinely fresh account.** Persona: Max, 31, Berlin, content manager, builds side-projects from his phone, reads English fine, has never created an API key in his life. New account **vinc.hafner4@gmail.com** (created for this audit, **zero BYOK keys**, walked the real onboarding on prod).

> **What changed since v1:** v1 started Max already-logged-in on a stocked account, so it skipped the part of his life where the pain actually lives: signup and key setup. This time Max started from nothing. The good news: the onboarding is the best thing in the product. The bad news: it walks him, politely and beautifully, straight into a brick wall.

---

## Max's story (what actually happened)

Max sees the landing page on his phone over coffee. *"Tell it what you want. It ships. The cloud workshop for builders who don't wait for a laptop."* That's him — he doesn't own a laptop he likes, he lives on his phone. "Bring your own AI keys, push to GitHub, deploy to Vercel." He doesn't fully know what two of those mean but it sounds legit. **3-day trial, no credit card.** He taps *Start building free.* Good first impression — no card wall, no sales call.

He signs up and lands in *"Set up your workshop — how should Goblin talk to AI?"* And here Goblin does something almost nobody does well: it tells him, in plain German, *"Goblin doesn't run the model — you do. Bring a key, or let us walk you through getting a free one. Either way, you stay in control."* Two clear paths: **"I already have a key"** (60 sec) or **"I'm new to this"** (~2 min, RECOMMENDED, guided, free). Max is exactly "new to this," so he taps the recommended one. Step 2: *"Pick your AI provider. Start with Gemini — Goblin handles the rest."* The top card is **Google Gemini, free tier, 1,500 requests/day, no card, ~60 sec setup**, with step-by-step instructions: go to aistudio.google.com, sign in, "Get API key → Create," paste it back. This is the first time a "developer tool" has ever made Max feel like the smart one in the room. He's genuinely impressed. He gets the key. He pastes it.

Then he types his first real thing — *"a landing page for my newsletter"* — and it dies. With the recommended Gemini provider he gets a model error; on the dashboard the default model is "Gemini Flash" and it says **"Model not found."** Max did *everything right*. He followed the recommended path, got the free key like he was told, and the product broke on the one action the whole landing page promised: "tell it what you want." In v1 Max would've quit at the first error message. In v2 it's worse in a specific way — he invested two minutes and a Google key *first*, so the failure feels like a betrayal, not a glitch. **He would not try a second time, and he'd tell the one friend who recommended it that it "doesn't work yet."**

---

## Findings (with Max's reaction)

### 🔴 Critical — the onboarding's recommended destination is broken
- The **#1 recommended provider** (Google Gemini 2.5 Pro / free Flash) is precisely the model that errors. A keyless newbie — the *exact* user this onboarding was built for — cannot reach a single working generation by following the guided path.
- Max's reaction: *"Ich hab alles gemacht wie es dastand. Den Key geholt, eingefügt, und dann 'Model not found.' Also gut, dann halt nicht."*
- **The cruel irony:** the better the onboarding, the more invested Max is when it fails. Goblin built a beautiful funnel into a dead model.

### 🔴 Critical — Max has no path to the model that *does* work
- The only working model is Groq Llama 3.3 70B, reachable by *manually overriding* the model picker. Onboarding never points him there. Max doesn't know "Groq" from "Gronk." He will never discover the working path on his own.

### 🟢 Preserve — the onboarding itself (this is a genuine asset)
- Plain-language framing ("Goblin doesn't run the model — you do"), no jargon in the setup copy, no card, the "we'll walk you through getting a free one" path, encrypted-keys reassurance, concrete key-acquisition steps. **Do not touch this except to fix where it points.** Vincent's claim that he "built a top setup assistant" is *true*.

### 🟠 Serious — dev jargon still leaks once you're past onboarding
- The moment something breaks, the polish evaporates: **"Model not found in LiteLLM"** and **"LLM Provider NOT provided"** are raw developer strings. Max reads "LiteLLM" and "Provider" and feels stupid — the exact feeling onboarding spent two minutes removing.
- The dashboard model chip says **"Gemini Flash"** — fine — but errors expose internal plumbing. Plain-German error copy is still missing where it matters most.

### 🟡 Minor
- Keyboard-shortcut hints ("⌘K", "⌘B") show even though Max is conceptually on a phone — meaningless to him.
- The shortcuts overlay floats over content and won't stay closed.

---

## Does the onboarding solve v1's BYOK pain?

**Half.** It solves *comprehension* — Max understands what a key is and how to get one, painlessly. It does **not** solve *outcome* — the key he's guided to get powers a broken model. Onboarding UX: **9/10.** Onboarding outcome: **0/10**, because the destination 400s. A 9/10 funnel into a 0/10 endpoint is a 0/10 product for Max.

---

## Verdict

**Would Max use Goblin instead of Claude + copy-paste?** Today, no — and now it's an *informed* no, from a real run, not an error screen. But for the first time the answer is *one bug away from yes*: if the recommended Gemini path produced a working generation, Max's walk would be **landing → signup → guided key → "newsletter page" → it appears → publish → live URL on his phone.** That walk is genuinely magic, and every piece of it except the model already works. Fix where onboarding points (or default new users to the working Groq path), put plain-German on the error states, and Max stays.

**One sentence to the founder:** You built the best onboarding I've seen for non-developers and then aimed it at the one model that doesn't work — repoint it at Groq and Max ships his newsletter tonight.
